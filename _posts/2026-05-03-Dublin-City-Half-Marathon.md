---
title: Dublin City Half Marathon 2026
location: Dublin, Co. Dublin.
---

On Sunday, May 3rd 2026, Dublin City Centre came alive with the second edition of the <b>Dublin City Half Marathon</b>. With <b>over 13,200 participants</b> registered for the sold-out event, the streets of the capital were packed with runners from across Ireland and beyond.

<b>Perfect Running Conditions</b><br>
Mother Nature delivered ideal racing conditions for the 21.1km course. The weather was cool and calm with <b>no headwind</b> to challenge the athletes, described by the women's winner as "perfect conditions" that allowed runners to maintain steady pace throughout. Temperatures remained comfortable from the 8:30am start on O'Connell Street through to the final finishers.

<b>The Scenic Route</b><br>
The course took runners on a spectacular tour of north Dublin, starting in the heart of the city and passing through <b>Phibsborough, Drumcondra, Beaumont, Artane, Killester, Raheny, Dollymount</b> and <b>Clontarf</b>, with the beautiful <b>St. Anne's Park</b> along the way, before returning via Fairview Park to finish on <b>Guild Street</b> near the iconic Luke Kelly statue.

<b>Elite Winners</b><br>
Seán Tobin of Clonmel Athletic Club dominated the men's race from the 2km mark, finishing in an impressive <b>63:11</b>. Paul O'Donnell (Dundrum) secured second place just 32 seconds back, with John Travers (Donore Harriers) third in 63:54.

In the women's race, Sorcha Nic Dhomhnaill from West Limerick Athletic Club claimed victory in <b>71:25</b>, successfully breaking the 72-minute barrier on her return to racing after maternity leave. Maebh Brannigan (Galway City Harriers) finished second in 74:47, with Sheila O'Byrne (Wicklow) third in 76:43.
